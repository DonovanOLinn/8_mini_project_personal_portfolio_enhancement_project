About Me

I’m a creative Brazilian-American Jew born and raised in Southern California. I use my art to build empathy through immersive and emergent storytelling & create a roadmap for healthy communications. I’m particularly interested in exploring community & destigmatize mental health conditions through genre-based multi-media.

I’m not a musician, but I play the funny bone and the heartstrings. As evidenced by the previous sentence, I’m incredibly corny.

My work blends sentiment, humor, interactivity, subversive use of genre, wordplay, & playfulness - making for unmistakably sweet, goofy, & surprising experiences.

My producing work in documentary films has been critically acclaimed. In recent memory, films I produced The Queen of Basketball (2022) and The Last Repair Shop (2023) both respectively won the Academy Award for Best Documentary (Short Subject). My screenplay Snow Day (2019) was selected for Austin Film Festival’s Script Competition - as a Second Rounder. My tabletop game An Arm & A Leg (2022) won the Dice Tower West Board Game Design Competition Champion and my party drawing game Disasterpieces is slated to come out through Skybound Entertainment in 2025.

I’m excited to face the challenges of integrating new programming skills into my creative works.

My Mission

Through my work, I aim to bring clarity, joy, and connectivity to people's lives every day.
